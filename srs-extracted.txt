1.INTRODUCTION
This document outlines the Software Requirements Specification (SRS) for a Domestic Violence Reporting and Supporting System aimed at assisting people in Ethiopia who are experiencing risk of domestic violence. Our System called Safe Haven which is a web-based platform designed to facilitate streamlined reporting and support for individuals affected by domestic violence. This system, intended for use by community support organizations and individuals in need, will enhance communication, provide resources, and ensure swift and secure responses through a user-friendly interface.
The SafeHaven platform will support users by offering a confidential environment where they can report incidents of domestic violence, request assistance, access information on legal and medical resources, and connect with support services. It will include features such as appointment scheduling for consultations, contact options, and personalized support services. The platform will also offer users access to information on protective measures, shelter services, and medical and legal aid.
The SRS document serves as the foundation for both front-end and back-end development, detailing the system's functional and non-functional requirements to ensure all user needs are addressed effectively. This document will outline system specifications, including a use case diagram that clarifies the interactions between users, support agents, and administrators. By defining the scope and functionality of the system, the SRS will guide quality assurance processes and allow users and support agents to validate that SafeHaven meets its defined requirements and objectives..
Purpose
This Software Requirement Specification document has been written to advance the ideas that have been presented in the project proposal. Although it may be referred to by less technically informed users, it remains a system requirement specification document. Therefore, the primary purpose of this document is to serve as the requirement list to aid in the design and implementation processes of the project.
As is typical for SRS documents, this file does not contain information about any design and implementation details. Any specified figures are mere placeholders and are subject to change. For concrete details regarding the design and architecture, the design documentation is recommended. Its functional requirements describe all the necessary features that the complete project will have. There may be several revisions of this document in the future, so the reader is advised to refer to the latest available version as it will best reflect the final product.

2.GENERAL DESCRIPTION
product perspective
The Domestic Violence Reporting and Supporting System (DVRSS) is web-based application designed to address the critical issue of domestic violence in Ethiopia. This system aims to provide a secure and accessible platform for victims to report incidents, access support services, and engage with law enforcement and social workers. By recognizing the unique cultural, legal, and technological landscape of Ethiopia, the DVRSS seeks to empower individuals in both urban and rural areas, ensuring that all victims have the resources they need to seek help and support.
The DVRSS will feature user-friendly interfaces for various stakeholders, including victims, social workers, and law enforcement personnel, promoting ease of use and accessibility. With functionalities such as secure messaging, and comprehensive resource directories, the system will enhance the overall experience for users while maintaining strict confidentiality and data protection standards. By fostering collaboration among service providers and ensuring a responsive support network, the DVRSS aims to create a safer environment for individuals affected by domestic violence.
Product Functions
The DVRSS encompasses a range of functionalities tailored to meet the diverse needs of its users.
Incident Reporting
One of the primary functions of the system is incident reporting. Victims of domestic violence can report incidents either anonymously or with identification, depending on their comfort level and safety concerns. The system allows users to provide detailed descriptions of incidents, including the type of violence experienced, location, and details about the involved parties. This feature is crucial as it not only facilitates accurate reporting but also helps authorities understand the context of each case. Furthermore,
the capability for victims to upload supporting documentation or evidence—such as photos or videos—enhances the credibility of reports and aids in subsequent investigations.
Support Resource Portal
In addition to reporting capabilities, the DVRSS will feature a robust support resource portal. This portal will serve as a comprehensive directory of available services tailored specifically for victims of domestic violence. It will include contact information for local authorities, healthcare providers, legal professionals, and shelters, ensuring that victims can easily access vital resources. Emergency help seeker users issue will be prominently displayed at the top of the system, allowing users to seek immediate assistance when needed. By providing a centralized platform for support services, the DVRSS aims to reduce barriers to accessing help.
Case Management
Effective case management is another critical component of the DVRSS. Law enforcement officers and social workers will have access to reported cases, enabling them to update the status of investigations or interventions seamlessly. The system will incorporate alerts and notifications that inform stakeholders when new reports are submitted or when action is required. This functionality ensures timely responses to incidents and fosters collaboration among various agencies involved in supporting victims.
User Management and Access Control
To maintain confidentiality and ensure user safety, the DVRSS will implement a robust user management and access control system. Different roles will be defined within the system, including victims, social workers, law enforcement personnel, and administrators. Access to sensitive information will be restricted based on user roles, safeguarding the privacy of victims while allowing authorized personnel to perform their duties effectively.
User Characteristics
The DVRSS is designed with a diverse user base in mind, encompassing several primary groups:
Victims: The system primarily targets victims of domestic violence, Specially people who may be unfamiliar with technology. Many users may have access to mobile phones or public kiosks but require an interface that is simple and easy to navigate. The design will prioritize confidentiality and security, ensuring that victims feel safe when using the platform.
Social Workers and Support Staff: Social workers and support staff are t professionals who will interact with the DVRSS to manage cases, offer support, and track progress. They will require more advanced
functionalities to update case information effectively and communicate with other stakeholders involved in victim support.
Law Enforcement: Law enforcement officers will need secure access to incident reports and updates for investigation purposes. The DVRSS must provide them with the necessary tools to access sensitive data while maintaining stringent security measures to protect victim information.
System Administrators: System administrators play a crucial role in managing the overall health of the DVRSS. They will be responsible for overseeing system settings, user roles, and ensuring that all functionalities operate smoothly. Administrators will require full access to all system features to perform their duties effectively.
General Constraints
The design and development of the DVRSS will be subject to several constraints that must be carefully considered:
Regulatory Policies: Compliance with local and international laws regarding data privacy is paramount, particularly concerning the protection of minors and victims of domestic violence. The system must adhere to these regulations to ensure that it operates within legal frameworks while safeguarding user information.
Hardware Limitations: Given that many users may rely on low-resource devices such as older smartphones or have limited internet connectivity, the DVRSS must be designed to function efficiently on these platforms. This consideration is essential for ensuring accessibility for a wide range of users, particularly those in rural areas.
Interface with Other Applications: To facilitate seamless coordination and data sharing among various agencies, the DVRSS may need to integrate with existing government databases or other social service management systems. This requirement necessitates careful planning during development to ensure compatibility with external systems.
Assumptions and Dependencies
Several assumptions underpin the successful implementation of the DVRSS:
Internet Access: It is assumed that internet access is generally available in urban areas and that users can access mobile phones even in rural locations.
Availability of Support Services: The system assumes that legal and social support services will be available to assist victims effectively.
Cooperation from Local Authorities: Successful functioning of the DVRSS depends on the cooperation of local law enforcement and healthcare providers in responding promptly to reported incidents.
Stable Operating Systems: The system will rely on stable operating systems for mobile platforms (Android and iOS) to ensure that users can access the platform without technical difficulties.

3.1.2.Hardware Interfaces
The hardware interface for the domestic violence report and service system is designed to be straightforward and easy to implement. The System supports mobile devices as the primary hardware, with optional support for wearable like smartwatches. Connectivity is maintained via Wi-Fi or cellular networks (4G/5G, with 3G fallback), and GPS is used for location tracking only when necessary. Security measures include HTTPS for encrypted communication and simple authentication (PIN or password) for user access. To ensure compatibility, the system integrates with common devices using standard libraries and SDKs, keeping the interface accessible and manageable for new developers.Key considerations include minimal battery usage, straightforward updates, and secure data storage to protect user privacy.

Use Case
A use case is a detailed description that outlines how people interact with a system. It captures the specific steps and actions that users take to accomplish a particular task, along with how the system responds at each step. This helps everyone involved in building the system understand what needs to be done and ensures that all user needs are met. By breaking down the process into simple, relatable scenarios, use cases make it easy for both technical and non-technical people to see how the system should work and identify any potential issues early on.
Figure 1: Use Case for SafeHaven platform
User Logs Into App
The user begins by logging into the application. If they are a new user, they start by signing up, which involves entering their user information, submitting this information, and having it validated and confirmed by the system. A confirmation email is then sent to the user, who needs to verify their email address. For existing users, the login process involves entering their email and password, submitting the login details, and having the credentials validated by the system to access the dashboard. The profile management section allows users to update their personal information and preferences, save changes, and receive a confirmation message.
Report Incident
When reporting an incident, the user fills in the incident details, including the type of violence, location, time, and description. The user then submits the report, which the system verifies and confirms by sending a confirmation message. The system also provides real-time updates on the case status and next steps. The report is then sent to the appropriate authorities or support team. Throughout this process, the user receives updates on the case status and the next steps to take.
Request Support
For requesting support, the user selects their preferred support option, such as chatting with a counselor. The system then connects the user with a support staff member, who provides real-time guidance and resources. If no support staff are available, the system notifies the user and provides emergency contact information or resources. In cases where immediate emergency help is needed, the system contacts emergency services directly.
View Dashboard
The dashboard allows users to view the status of their reports and support requests. Users can also see recent activities related to their cases and receive real-time notifications and updates on the dashboard. This feature provides a comprehensive overview of all interactions and case statuses, ensuring that users are kept informed.
Admin Functions
Admin functions include managing users, reviewing user activity, searching and reviewing users, monitoring the system, saving changes, and logging out. These functions help maintain the overall system and ensure that user activities are monitored and managed effectively.
Support Staff Functions
Support staff functions involve accessing the dashboard, responding to incident reports, connecting with victims, reviewing and managing support requests, providing guidance and resources, and logging out. These functions enable support staff to efficiently assist victims and manage their support needs.

3.4 Non-Functional Requirements
3.4.1 Performance
Concurrent Users: The system must effectively handle multiple users simultaneously without experiencing latency. It should be capable of supporting concurrent users during peak operation hours.
Response Time: Aim for a consistent response time of less than 10 seconds under normal operating conditions to ensure a seamless and efficient user experience. This includes quick load times for reports and support requests.
Notification Delay: Minimize delays for notifications and messages to under 5 second. Notifications regarding incident updates, support replies, and system alerts should be instantaneous to enhance user engagement.
Load Handling: The system should dynamically scale to handle increased loads during high traffic situations, prioritizing critical functions such as incident reporting and emergency assistance. Utilize load balancing techniques to manage server requests effectively.
3.4.2 Reliability
Uptime-Ensure: the system is available 90% of the time to maximize user accessibility and avoid potential lapses during critical reporting periods.
Resilience: The system should recover quickly from minor outages with minimal impact on users, allowing for continuous processing and interaction. Implement automated recovery protocols that restore service within minutes.
Error Logging and Monitoring: Comprehensive logging and monitoring systems should be established to capture errors and system performance metrics. This enables proactive identification of issues, facilitating faster response times from support teams.
3.4.3 Availability
24/7 Access: The system should be consistently available 24/7, enabling users to report incidents or access support at any time of day or night. This is crucial, particularly in emergency situations.
Downtime Notifications: Users should be informed in advance about any planned downtime, including the expected duration and time of service resumes. Notifications should be sent via email and within the application to ensure users are adequately informed.
Backup Systems: Establish robust redundant systems, including database and server fail over options, to maintain service availability during unplanned outages. Regularly test these backups to ensure functionality during emergencies.
3.4.4 Security
Data Encryption: All data transmissions should be encrypted using industry-standard protocols such as TLS to protect sensitive user information from potential threats. This includes data both in transit and at rest.
Multi-Factor Authentication: Implement reliable multi-factor authentication (MFA) processes that require users to verify their identity through multiple means such as a text message code or authentication app to enhance account security.
Privacy Compliance: Ensure strict adherence to privacy laws, such as GDPR and HIPAA, protecting user information and maintaining compliance with legal standards. Regular audits should be conducted to ensure ongoing compliance.
Regular Security Audits: Schedule regular security assessments, vulnerability scans, and penetration testing to identify and mitigate potential threats. Utilize third-party security experts to conduct these assessments for an unbiased view.
3.4.5 Maintainability
Easy Updates: Design the system architecture for easy updates and bug fixes, enabling developers to implement changes without significant downtime or disruption to services. Automated testing should ensure stability post-update.
Modular Code: Use a modular code structure and adhere to standard coding practices to simplify maintenance, promote re-usability, and enable collaborative development across teams. This allows for isolated updates and feature additions.
Documentation: Maintain comprehensive documentation across all levels of the system development, including API definitions, user guides, and architectural decisions, to support future development, on boarding of new developers, and troubleshooting.
3.4.6 Portability
Device Compatibility: The application must perform seamlessly across multiple devices and operating systems, including recent versions of  iOS, Android, and web browsers. This includes ensuring a consistent user interface (UI) and experience (UX) across platforms.
Wide Reach: Users should be able to access the system from various devices (smartphones, tablets, laptops, and desktops) without performance issues, enabling flexibility and convenience in reporting and support tasks.
Cross-Browser Support: Ensure compatibility with major web browsers (such as Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge) through rigorous testing. This aids in maximizing accessibility for web users and ensuring that no portion of the user base is excluded due to browser limitations.
Localization: Support multiple languages and regional settings to cater to diverse user demographics, ensuring that language barriers do not impede access to reporting and support services.
3.5  Inverse Requirements
The domestic violence reporting and support system does not include the following:
Limited Geographic Coverage
Primarily Includes Organizations Located in the Capital City: The system focuses on resources and support services such as law firms, psychological clinics, and hospitals that are based in the capital city. Users outside this area may not find relevant local resources within the platform, at least for the time being. Additionally, the main database is planned to be kept in one sub-city, specifically the Arada Kefele Ketema Women and Child Office.
Monolingual Support
Not Multilingual: The platform is not designed to support multiple languages. Users who do not speak or understand the primary language of the system may find it challenging to navigate. Nevertheless, the system is designed to be easy to navigate and user-friendly, with familiar layouts and clear instructions to help users access and utilize the services effectively, even if they do not speak the language fluently.
Lack of Real-Time Support
No Real-Time Assistance: Users who report domestic violence may not receive immediate support because it takes time to authorize and start the work. The system is not equipped to provide instant service at the moment of reporting.
Inability to Identify Fake Reports
No Mechanism to Distinguish Genuine from Fake Reports: The system does not include means to verify whether a report is real or fake. As a result, there is no built-in functionality to differentiate between genuine and false reports.
3.6  Design Constraints
Privacy and Security Policies: User roles and permissions must comply with strict privacy policies to ensure sensitive data is accessible only to authorized personnel
Robust encryption protocols and secure authentication methods are required to protect user data.
Device Compatibility: The platform should be compatible with a wide range of devices, including smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers.
Core functions (e.g., incident reporting, access to resources) must perform efficiently on mobile hardware, which may limit certain high-performance functionalities.
Data Storage and Compliance: Storing user data and reports securely while complying with data protection regulations poses a significant challenge. Implementing data encryption and secure storage solutions that meet local and international laws is essential but can be complex and costly.
Maintenance and Updates: The system should be designed for easy maintenance and updates, allowing for the seamless addition of new features or fixes without significant downtime. Ensuring this flexibility can be complex in a large-scale system.

Appendix I
Observational Visit to Arada Kefele Ketema Women and Child Office
As part of our preparatory research for the project, our team conducted an observational visit to the Arada Kefele Ketema Women and Child Office to gain insights into the existing systems and understand how people report their issues and how these issues are addressed. Our objective was to identify the current workflows, comprehend the operational challenges faced by the staff when interacting with clients, and gather requirements needed for the system from the perspective of the staff (the authorities there).
Figure 2:Arada Kefele Ketema Women and Child Office.
During the visit, we had the opportunity to converse with one of the authorities at the Arada Kefele Ketema Women and Child Office. Based on this conversation, we noted the following problems:
Challenges in Providing Support: The office faces considerable challenges in assisting individuals who come to report incidents. Often, it is difficult even to cover the transportation costs for these individuals, which significantly hinders the overall support process.
Difficulties in Establishing Connections with Legal Entities: Connecting reporters (individuals who report incidents) with the appropriate legal frameworks poses significant challenges. This connection is essential for providing the necessary assistance and ensuring that subsequent actions are effectively carried out.
The challenges and difficulties observed during our visit have inspired us to develop a centralized system. This system will allow individuals to access the necessary services after a single sign-up. By creating a centralized platform, we aim to streamline the process, ensuring that individuals do not have to repeatedly provide their information and can efficiently access the services they need. This solution will not only improve the overall user experience but also enhance the coordination and effectiveness of the support provided by the office. By connecting individuals with the appropriate resources and legal frameworks through a unified system, we can address the issues more efficiently and ensure timely assistance for those in need.
Appendix II
Survey Data
We conducted a survey involving different members of society to observe and gauge their insights and understanding about the issue, as well as to receive comments in order to make the system relatable and usable for them.
From the survey, we observed that most people have experienced or know someone who has experienced domestic violence. Nevertheless, most of these individuals do not know how and where to report such incidents. Referring to the survey results, we found that the majority of respondents are comfortable reporting these incidents through a web application.
The survey provided us with valuable insights, leading us to add educational content to the website. This content will inform users about the necessary steps to take and where to go in such situations, as well as how they can guide and assist victims of violence even if they do not personally experience it. Additionally, we included phone numbers and hot-lines of willing professionals for individuals who seek psychological support.
